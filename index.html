Glass segmentation and reconstruction

Abstract

Transparent objects are particularly challenging due to their lack of texture and features and the drastic changes in appearance they suffer as the background, illumination and camera pose change. This problem has been previously addressed by using structured light and polarized light, depth sensors or by analyzing the distortions produced by the transparent object to the background. These methods require a carefully controlled environment, specialized hardware or might suffer of lack of information when a single image provide weak visual cues for the detection of such kind of objects.

We explore the use of learning methods combined with super-pixel segmentation to improve the analysis of single frames of a video sequence containing transparent objects. We use transparent rigid objects such as glass and plastic, from which we extract small image patches using graph-based segmentation that are used within a supervised learning method. We also integrate multiple views from a video stream to improve object detection when the conditions from a single view are not favorable. This method is a base for future work on modeling of transparent objects using color images.

Sponsors

Ixnamiki & Ixnamiki Öme Rescue Robots

Ixnamiki and Ixnamiki Öme are two exploration and rescue robot designed and developed by engineering students at the Universidad Panamericana campus Bonaterra in Aguascalientes México.

The word Ixnamiki is a nahuatl verb that translates to "to find people", Öme is the nahuatl word for "the second".

Ixnamiki Rescue Robot was originally designed to participate on the "Exploration and Rescue League" of the RoboCup (a international robotics competition). It has participated on the RoboCup Singapore 2010, IranOpen 2010, RoboCup Istambul 2011, RoboCup Mexico City 2012 and RoboCup 2013.
